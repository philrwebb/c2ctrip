Perfect—being comfortable with **20–25 km days** fits the **Coast to Coast in 10 walking days** very well. Below is a slightly tuned version of the 10‑day itinerary that keeps each day broadly in that 20–25 km range, with a few “lighter” days where terrain is tough (e.g., Lakes climbs).  

***

### 10‑day C2C itinerary (20–25 km comfort zone)

1. **Day 1 – St Bees → Ennerdale Bridge**  
   - Distance: ~22–24 km.  
   - Start at the Irish Sea, climb over High Stile, then descend into Ennerdale; classic opening day.[1][2]

2. **Day 2 – Ennerdale Bridge → Wasdale Head**  
   - Distance: ~16–18 km.  
   - Heavier but shorter day; great views of Scafell and Great Gable.[2][1]

3. **Day 3 – Wasdale Head → Rosthwaite (Borrowdale)**  
   - Distance: ~22–24 km.  
   - Over Sty Head; one of the classic “big” days in the central Lakes.[1][2]

4. **Day 4 – Rosthwaite → Patterdale**  
   - Distance: ~20–22 km.  
   - Around Grasmere and down to the Ullswater end; still mountainous but slightly easier than the previous day.[3][2]

5. **Day 5 – Patterdale → Pooley Bridge**  
   - Distance: ~16–18 km.  
   - Gentle Ullswater‑side walking; good “recovery” day before the Eden Valley.[2][3]

6. **Day 6 – Pooley Bridge → Brough**  
   - Distance: ~22–24 km.  
   - Onto the Pennines; a good long day, but mostly on lower, rolling fells.[1][2]

7. **Day 7 – Brough → Kirkby Stephen**  
   - Distance: ~14–16 km.  
   - Shorter, easier day; nice transition into the Yorkshire Dales proper.[2][1]

8. **Day 8 – Kirkby Stephen → Keld**  
   - Distance: ~20–22 km.  
   - Through Swaledale, with characterful stone villages.[1][2]

9. **Day 9 – Keld → Reeth**  
   - Distance: ~20–22 km.  
   - Continue along Swaledale; still within your 20–25 km sweet spot.[2][1]

10. **Day 10 – Reeth → Robin Hood’s Bay (via Sutton Bank / Blakey Ridge)**  
    - Distance: ~24–26 km (could be pushed to 28 km if you want a full‑on finale).  
    - Long moorland and coastal‑cliff walk; this will be your longest day but still manageable for you.[1][2]

***

### Where to put an extra rest day (optional)

If you later decide you want **11 days on the trail**, the best places to add a rest day are:
- **Wasdale Head** (after the Sty Head day, to recover and soak up the views).[3][2]
- **Kirkby Stephen** (before the Dales middle‑stretch).[3][2]

***

### How the fourth person moves baggage

Since you’re comfortable with these distances, your “fourth” (baggage‑mover) can either:
- Use a **luggage‑transfer company** (e.g., Coast to Coast Packhorse or Sherpa Van) and simply meet the group at the next B&B.[4][5]
- Or drive a **rental car/van** and move bags between stops, which is often cheaper and more flexible for a group of four.[5][6]
