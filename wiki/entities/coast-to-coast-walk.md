## Overview

The Coast to Coast Walk is a long-distance crossing of northern England that runs from St Bees on the Irish Sea to Robin Hood's Bay on the North Sea, passing through the Lake District, Yorkshire Dales, and North York Moors. The path was formally opened as a National Trail in March 2026.

## Characteristics

- Commonly described in the sources as roughly 190 to 192 miles, or about 309 to 313 km
- Now managed within a National Trail framework with partnership-led standards and investment
- Usually planned as a multi-day staged walk rather than a continuous through-hike with camping
- Contains a harder western section in the Lake District and generally less technical eastern sections after the major mountain crossings
- Supports multiple itinerary shapes, from a strong 10-day schedule to a more typical 14-day schedule and longer relaxed versions
