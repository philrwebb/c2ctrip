## Definition

Walking season selection is the choice of travel window for the [[entities/coast-to-coast-walk]] based on weather, daylight, terrain conditions, crowding, and business availability along the route.

## How It Works

The sources recommend late April through early October as the viable season, with May to June presented as the strongest compromise between pleasant conditions and manageable crowds. Winter is discouraged because of closures, short days, and more serious footing on exposed sections.

## Key Parameters

- Daylight hours
- Rain, wind, and storm risk
- Residual snow or cold on higher ground
- Accommodation opening patterns
- Peak-season crowding and pricing

## When To Use

Use this concept at the start of planning, before committing to accommodation or transport, because the chosen month affects both route conditions and trip cost.

## Risks & Pitfalls

- Assuming midsummer is automatically the best option
- Underestimating early-season cold on high passes
- Ignoring that some support services and lodging may be limited outside the main walking window
