## Definition

Luggage transfer support is the arrangement that lets walkers on the [[entities/coast-to-coast-walk]] carry only a day pack while bags are moved between overnight stops by a service or support person.

## How It Works

The sources describe two common models: using a commercial baggage-transfer provider or having a fourth person drive bags between lodging stops. Both models reduce carried weight and make faster itineraries easier to sustain. The newer source also emphasizes how baggage services help redistribute lodging choices when high-demand villages are full.

## Key Parameters

- Whether each overnight stop is supported by transfer services
- Cost and booking lead time
- Reliability of handoff timing
- Need for a dedicated support driver
- Flexibility if the walking plan changes mid-route
- Ability to route around accommodation bottlenecks

## When To Use

Use this concept when the group wants lighter packs, includes mixed fitness levels, or is attempting a faster schedule that would be harder with full overnight loads.

## Risks & Pitfalls

- Picking accommodation that does not align with the transfer plan
- Depending on a support driver without accounting for parking or route access
- Assuming baggage support removes the need for realistic pacing
- Treating baggage services as guaranteed without checking provider coverage by stop
