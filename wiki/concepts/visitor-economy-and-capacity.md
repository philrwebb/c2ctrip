## Definition

Visitor economy and capacity describes how trail demand on the [[entities/coast-to-coast-walk]] supports rural businesses while also stressing finite local infrastructure such as beds, transport links, and trail-adjacent services.

## How It Works

Trail users spend heavily on accommodation, food, and support services, creating strong local economic dependency. At the same time, high demand concentrates into small settlements, producing bottlenecks, higher prices, and stronger reliance on logistics providers like baggage-transfer companies and shuttle support.

## Key Parameters

- Through-hiker and day-user volume
- Bed capacity by settlement and season
- Service mix (pubs, shops, transfer providers, transport links)
- Price pressure during peak demand
- Ability of nearby towns to absorb overflow demand

## When To Use

Use this concept when judging itinerary viability, accommodation booking risk, and how policy or marketing changes might shift pressure between villages and larger service hubs.

## Risks & Pitfalls

- Assuming demand growth is always positive without capacity expansion
- Ignoring distribution effects where one village overloads while others underutilize capacity
- Planning late in the season window and expecting last-minute single-night availability
