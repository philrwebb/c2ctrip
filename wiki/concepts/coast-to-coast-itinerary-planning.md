## Definition

Coast to Coast itinerary planning is the process of breaking the [[entities/coast-to-coast-walk]] into realistic walking days, overnight stops, and travel logistics that match the group's fitness and trip goals.

## How It Works

The route is usually staged by combining terrain difficulty, available accommodation hubs, and arrival or departure constraints. The sources in this repo show a mainstream 14 to 16 stage pattern and a more aggressive 10-day pattern, both of which work by redistributing long and short days rather than keeping every stage identical. The lowland section between Richmond and the western edge of the North York Moors is a known operational risk if treated as a single overlong day.

## Key Parameters

- Total trip length in days
- Overnight stop locations
- Hard-versus-easy day sequencing
- Group fitness and pack weight
- Transport and baggage support options
- Bottleneck settlements and bed availability

## When To Use

Use this concept when deciding whether the walk should be fast, standard, or relaxed; when mapping overnight stops; and when coordinating accommodation or a support driver.

## Risks & Pitfalls

- Copying a generic itinerary without checking whether its daily stages match the group's pace
- Underestimating how much the early Lake District terrain slows progress
- Choosing stops based only on distance rather than accommodation or transport practicality
- Keeping historically overlong lowland transitions in one stage when splitting improves sustainability
